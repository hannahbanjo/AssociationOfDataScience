**Status:** Season-final regular-season statistics (data locked as of 2026-02-27; bracket seedings are pre-final pending March 1 remaining games as noted in bracket.json)

Both files are local fallbacks. No live web scraping was performed. The CSV was generated by `scripts/build_data.py`, which scrapes advanced school and opponent stats from Sports Reference (sports-reference.com) and joins them on team name. Friendly names are applied: "Louisiana State" → "LSU", "Mississippi" → "Ole Miss".

**Note:** The bracket.json itself flags: *"Re-run after March 1 regular season games to finalize."* Seedings are therefore preliminary and may shift slightly for teams tied on conference record. Downstream agents should treat seeds as current best estimates, not final.

### Caveats and Flags
1. **Texas A&M game count (24 games):** All other teams played 27–30 games. This is the lowest in the field and may slightly underweight their metrics due to smaller sample size. Downstream agents should note this when assessing Texas A&M.
2. **Preliminary seedings:** bracket.json notes seedings should be re-run after March 1. With three three-way ties (seeds 6/7/8 and 9/10 resolved by SRS) and remaining games pending, there is a small risk of seed re-ordering. Downstream agents should flag this uncertainty.
3. **SOS discrepancy for LSU vs. South Carolina:** LSU's SOS (11.11) is notably lower than South Carolina's (16.66) and Texas's (16.39). LSU's elite Net Efficiency (+45.8) and SRS (46.93) must be evaluated in that context by the Upset Detector and Matchup Analyst.
4. **Missouri negative Net Efficiency:** Missouri's DRtg (103.7) is the only DRtg above 100 in the field, meaning opponents average over a point per possession against them. Their ORtg (98.7) does not compensate. They are statistically the weakest team in the field by this metric.
