Storylines to Watch

1. The LSU Seed Anomaly: The Best Team in the Field Is the Fourth Seed

By every efficiency metric — Net Efficiency (+45.8, best in field), ORtg (121.8, best in field), and SRS (46.93, second in field) — LSU is the best or second-best team in Greenville. They are seeded fourth because their conference record (11-4) trails the 12-3 marks of Texas and Vanderbilt. The catch: LSU's SOS of 11.11 is the second-lowest in the field. Two agents in this pipeline reach conflicting conclusions on the LSU-vs.-South Carolina semifinal. That disagreement is not reconciled here — it is the right question, and it will be answered on the court.

2. Tennessee's Ticking Clock: The Most Overseeded Team in the Field

Tennessee is a 7-seed with the statistical profile of an 11. Net Efficiency (+11.7) is the lowest of any team seeded 1–8. Their DRtg (89.8) is the worst among top-eight seeds. The defense: they played the hardest schedule in the entire field (SOS 19.77). But schedule credit evaporates at tipoff. Their three-point dependency (3PAr .457 — highest in the field) makes them one cold-shooting game away from elimination. Georgia in the Second Round will test them immediately.

3. Georgia's Quiet Case for a Semifinal Run

Georgia is a 10-seed that no one is talking about. Net Efficiency (+18.0) ranks eighth in the field — ahead of Tennessee, Alabama, and every other team seeded below them. DRtg (84.5) is the best of any team outside the top six seeds. The caveat: SOS (10.89) is second-lowest in the field. Both things are true simultaneously.

4. South Carolina vs. Texas: The Championship Game No One Should Argue With

South Carolina (ORtg 118.6, DRtg 76.1) and Texas (ORtg 116.3, DRtg 75.7) are statistically indistinguishable. The Net Efficiency gap is 1.9 points. Both teams lead the field in defensive efficiency and play interior-dominant basketball at identical paces. The Matchup Analyst gives South Carolina the edge solely on shooting efficiency (.587 vs. .559 TS%). Sub-Agent 2 calls it "a coin flip." This report agrees with both.
